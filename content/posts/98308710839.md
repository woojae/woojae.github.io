> When I decided to start learning web development, I took a “total immersion” approach. I listened to programming podcasts at the gym, filled my inbox with web development newsletters, and made HackerNews my homepage. [Thinkful’s](<http://www.thinkful.com/a/blog>) [“Best Of” Series](<http://blog.thinkful.com/search/bestof>) aims to help you do the same, by guiding you to resources that will bring you up to speed with the world of web development and inspire you to keep learning.
> 
> This week, we’ll share our favorite podcasts. Topics range from freelancing and business to mobile development! 
> 
> **Editor’s note:** Upon request, here’s an easy-to-remember link to share the podcast love :) bit.ly/thinkcast
> 
> ### **Design and Front-end** :
> 
> **[The Big Web Show](<https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/the-big-web-show/id370445683?mt=2>)**
> 
> Host: [Jeffrey Zeldman](<https://twitter.com/zeldman>)
> 
> Zeldman interviews a new guest each episode, covering a variety of tech topics with a particular focus on front-end design.
> 
> **[Boagworld Web Design Show](<http://boagworld.com/show/>)**
> 
> Hosts: [Paul Boag](<https://twitter.com/boagworld>) and [Marcus Lillington](<https://twitter.com/marcus67>)
> 
> A web design podcast that’s also “quintessentially British,” this show aims to provide useful information for web designers and developers at a variety of levels.
> 
> **[How to Hold a Pencil](<http://howtoholdapencil.com/>)**
> 
> Host: [Reuben Ingber](<https://twitter.com/ReubenIngber>)
> 
> Every week, Reuben interviews a self-taught developer or designer. Each story aims to help people explore different paths towards their programming education. Shout out to [Episode 14](<http://www.howtoholdapencil.com/episodes/014/>)!
